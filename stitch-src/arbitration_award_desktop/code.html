Executive Summary

Upon review of all submitted materials, evidentiary hearings, and witness testimonies regarding the breach of contract dispute between Horizon Logistics (Claimant) and Apex Manufacturing (Respondent), the tribunal finds in favor of the Claimant. The Respondent failed to meet the delivery obligations stipulated in Master Service Agreement (Ref: MSA-2021-04), resulting in direct financial damages.

fact_check Findings of Fact

  • 01.

    The Master Service Agreement (MSA) was executed on January 15, 2021, establishing a 24-month delivery schedule for critical industrial components.

  • 02.

    Respondent failed to deliver shipments Scheduled for Q3 and Q4 of 2022, citing supply chain disruptions as a Force Majeure event.

  • 03.

    Evidence presented by Claimant demonstrates that Respondent allocated available inventory to a higher-paying third-party client during the disputed period, invalidating the Force Majeure defense.

gavel Legal Conclusions

The tribunal concludes that the Respondent's actions constitute a material breach of Section 4.2 of the MSA. The defense of Force Majeure is rejected based on the evidence of willful reallocation of resources. Consequently, the Claimant is entitled to compensatory damages for lost revenue directly attributable to the breach, as well as reasonable attorney's fees pursuant to Section 12.1 of the agreement.

"A party cannot rely on a force majeure clause when the inability to perform is caused by the party's own voluntary actions or economic prioritization." - Ref: Arbitral Precedent 2019-A4
